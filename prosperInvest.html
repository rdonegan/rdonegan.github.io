Prosper Invest

OVERVIEW

Problem. Some mobile investors prefer more control over their portfolio and investments. How do we enable these investors without adding friction?

Constraints. The solution had to work for the existing Prosper Invest app, and comply with all finance regulations.

My role. I led research and design for the solution. I drew from established patterns and styles for the app: Prosper Invest.

Result. Created an experience for Prosper investors to review new investment options, and purchase from their smartphone.

THE PROBLEM

Prosper offers a platform that allows investors to partially fund the personal loans of others - think fractional investing, but for personal loans. In August 2019, Prosper launched the Prosper Invest app. The app allowed investors to create an investment portfolio, track their returns, and transfer cash. The app originally launched with an AutoInvest feature.

We heard from investors that AutoInvest was a convenient, and effective way for diversifying portfolios. However, we also heard feedback that some users wanted more control over their investments, and that AutoInvest didn't provide the granularity desired by some investors.

The ability to invest manually was already available for Investors on the web, but not without drawbacks of its own. We sought to understand the pain points of the existing experience, coupled with what our users were saying, to provide an optimized mobile experience for manual investors.

HOW CAN MOBILE HELP?

Mobile isnâ€™t a perfect medium, but in this case, it actually provided some noteworthy benefits:

  • Strict info hierarchy. Early discovery suggested that information overload is a huge deterrent for new and even intermediate investors. Limited mobile real estate reinforced the need to prioritize only the most critical information.
  • Familiar patterns. I knew that we could draw on familiar patterns from shopping apps to make our experience more approachable.
  • A touch of humanity. Delight and finance often don't go hand in hand, but at Prosper that isn't the case! I wanted to find subtle but meaningful ways to do this natively.

HOW TO MAKE INVESTING APPROACHABLE

Investors found our web experience clunky and complex. Even our most experienced investors found the interface dense, and difficult to navigate.

I tested a variety of ways to approach this mobile - knowing that simplicity was key. The most successful test was of a flow that mimicked mobile shopping. The patterns of adding to a cart, filtering results, and checking out really resonated.

INFORMATION CLARITY

Tackling the order of information took a lot of trial and error followed by user validation. I started by referring to the online experience, and working from there.

I explored both layout and content. Visually, I grappled with the ability to show more listings on the screen vs. easier comparison. Eventually, I opted for a single column card layout. This allowed users to more easily scan and compare listings.

Simplicity and approachability guided my work on content. I tried to incorporate that into my sketches by prioritizing the purpose of each loan first. Ultimately, I deprioritized this info in favor of listing grade and yield, which were universally valued.

FILTER AND SORT

Unsurprisingly, some of our experienced investors make great use of sort and filter. Our team identified the top-used filters, and simplified from 30+ on web to just 12 on our mobile experience.

UI TWEAKS

I was lucky to work an app with an established design system. I drew on styles and conventions throughout the rest of the app to influence my work. I added a few new styles to accommodate the shopping experience, but hoped the feature would fit seamlessly into the rest of the UI.

The manual invest experience naturally complemented our pre-existing AutoInvest feature. Each time an investor chooses to update their portfolio, they can choose to update their target allocations, or pick their investments manully.

WHAT I WOULD DO NEXT

There are a number of tweaks I'd like to make to this feature now that it's released. Here are a few:

  • Approachability and learning. The experience right now is catered mainly to intermediate and experienced investors. With a few tweaks, we can simplify complex copy and provide guidance to get novice users started.
  • Personalization and empathy. I explored a few different ways to make finance feel more human. With more fine-tuning, I think we can improve the personalization many of our investors appreciate.
  • More data. With more time, I'd like to understand how our users invest, and how we can more intelligently streamline that process.
