Prosper Invest

OVERVIEW

Problem. Some Prosper investors prefer more control over their portfolio. How do we enable these users, without hampering them with unnecessary detail?

Constraints. This feature needed to be integrated with our mobile application: Prosper Invest.

My role. I led research and design for the solution. A lot of the UI work was based on styles established by an earlier designer.

Result. Investors gained a new way to invest on the go, now available on iOs and Android.

THE PROBLEM

Prosper offers a unique model that allows peers to invest in the personal loans of others. While most Prosper users enjoy a passive investment strategy, some prefer a manual approach that allows them to pick and choose the personal loans into which they invest.

The challenge was to accommodate for both types of Prosper investors - passive and active - with our brand new mobile application.

HOW CAN MOBILE HELP?

Mobile isnâ€™t a perfect medium, but in my case, it actually provided some great benefits:

  • Forced Focus. With limited screen real estate, I focused on simplifying investment info in order to surface what matters most.
  • Established shopping patterns. I aimed to replicate familiar mobile shopping patterns to establish a trusted investment experience.
  • Micro-delight. Investments may not seem like a traditionally delightful space, but small moments of joy fit well within our brand. I knew I could unlock these moments in a way that I hoped would celebrate and encourage investments on the platform.

DESIGNING THE SHOPPING EXPERIENCE

I heard from active investors that our web experience was clunky and complex. I also knew that most of our active users are familiar with mobile shopping applications. I aimed to find a parallel between the simplicity of retail shopping and investing.

By user testing with customers similar to our own investors, I explored the general shopping cart flow, and how different verbiage translated in context.

INFORMATION HIERARCHY

From user research, I knew that essentials like project loan yield, investment amount, and investment purpose are important to prospective investors. I sought to display this information clearly, while also making the process feel more human.

FILTER AND SORT

Unsurprisingly, people who choose to invest manually prefer a wider range of control over that process. Still, the goal was to simplify, not overwhelm. I worked with product to identify the most popular filter and sort options on the web version of the experience, and prioritized those in the app.
