Prosper Invest

OVERVIEW

Problem. Investors struggle to find the invest functionality, and others do not understand Prosper's unique investment process.

Constraints. My team's work had to integrate with the Prosper Invest app, and comply with all financial regulations.

My Role. I was the sole designer on our mobile team and led UX and research. I worked with a PM and four developers to implement the final result.

Result. Our team built native invest functionality, and tweaked our IA to improve discovery of all investment features.

BACKGROUND

Prosper was the first company in the US to allow peer-to-peer lending and borrowing. In August 2019, Prosper launched Prosper Invest to allow allow investors to manage and add to their portfolios on-the-go.

LISTENING TO OUR USERS

After the app launched, I worked with out PM to monitor feedback. We drew on reviews from the appstore and feedback sent directly to Prosper.

Early feedback revealed two things:

  1. Purchasing individual investments was difficult. The app allowed users to invest automatically, but manual invest required logging into the web experience. Some users struggled to log in, and found the web experience tedious.
  2. Many couldn't find the investment functionality to begin with. Users claimed being unable to investment manually from the app.

We sought to understand this feedback more deeply to address it on mobile.

PREVIEW OF THE FINAL RESULT

Our team spent roughly two months investigating the problem and implementing a solution. By the end of our process, we had improved our app's information architecture (IA), and implemented native manual invest backed by user research.

OUR PROCESS

Before jumping to a solution, I highlighted to my team that we should use this opportunity to validate our assumptions and discover how our users actually invest. I outlined a flexible approach for how that could happen.

DISCOVERY

I conducted 2 user tests across 10 participants to observe our UI in practice. I worked with our PM to create tests that simulated investing on mobile or web. We aimed to answer several questions:

  • How do investors use Prosper Invest to invest today?
  • How do the web and mobile investment experiences differ for users?
  • Are there behavioral differences between active and passive investors?

User Testing Insights:

  1. Both experienced and novice investors found the web experience overwhelming. Experienced investors struggles to locate investment details, and novce investors were confused by some terminology.
  2. Investment content was more easily digested as a list. Participants compared key metrics like yield and investment grade.
  3. Mobile participants could not find the invest screen. 4 of 5 participants looked to the Account screen first when trying to update their investments.

INFORMATION ARCHITECTURE

The existing app has three main sections: Account for viewing performance over time, Transfer to add or deposit cash, and Portfolio to invest and update investment strategy.

When I mapped the user journey, I noticed users getting stuck in two places: viewing their account balance and looking at their investment list. Users expected to invest from these pages, but couldn't.

To solve our discovery problem, I recommended updating these pathways to link directly to invest. That way, we could test if we needed a bigger IA overhaul to expose the invest feature.

IDEATION

I assumed that investors were more comfortable with familiar patterns like online shopping. To test this assumption, I drafted low-fi concepts and put them in front of investors. I found that the patterns of adding to cart, filtering results, and checking out all resonated.

INFORMATION CLARITY

As investors browsed options, I observed that yield is the first "gate" investors need to pass to determine if a loan is worth their investment.

Next, some investors sought details on the borrower and financial history associated with each loan. For this reason, I knew it was important to prioritize information affecting the viability of an investment - yield and investment grade - while giving a clear pathway to all the granular details.

I explored both layout and content. Visually, I grappled with the ability to show more listings on the screen vs. easier comparison. Eventually, I opted for a single column card layout. This allowed users to more easily scan and compare listings.

SCANNERS AND POWER-SHOPPERS

Usage date from our web platform showed that roughly 30% of investors never look beyond the summary for each investment. The other 70% review each investment in detail before deciding to invest. I dubbed these personas Scanners and Power-Shoppers, respectively.

Scanners are primarily motivated by diversification, and rely mainly on the grade and yield of each option to invest. Power-shoppers on the other hand are particularly risk averse, and truly want to understand the likelihood a loan will be paid back at a more granular level.

My goal was to support Scanners who select many investments at once, while enabling Power-Shoppers to dive in for details on a loan and borrower.

FILTER AND SORT

I observed that investors used a small, specific set of filters to narrow down their search. I advocated for simplicity over complexity, and advocated we limit filters to the top used. Based on data, we ended up simplifying from 30+ filter and sort options to just 12 on mobile.

VISUAL DESIGN

Because Prosper Invest has an established design system, my goal was to visually evoke themes of simplicity, clarity, and delight. The UI is whitespace dominant with the exception of a light gray used on the (potentially long) list of investments. Blue is used to evoke attention and action, and the blue circle visual is carried through each loan listing. Upon a successful purchase, colorful confetti falls from the top of the screen.

PUTTING IT ALL TOGETHER

The manual invest experience naturally complemented our pre-existing AutoInvest feature. Each time an investor chooses to update their portfolio, they can choose to update their target allocations, or pick their investments manully.

TEST AND LEARN

We released the update in early April 2020! We continue to track feedback in a variety of formats including from the App Store and our own usage data. We'll be looking closely at trends in transaction size, investments per transaction, and any errors.

HOW MOBILE HELPED: A POST MORTEM

Mobile isnâ€™t a perfect medium, but in this case, it actually provided some noteworthy benefits:

  • Strict Info Hierarchy. Early discovery suggested that information overload is a huge deterrent for new and even intermediate investors. Limited mobile real estate reinforced the need to prioritize only the most critical information.
  • Familiar Patterns. Mobile shopping patterns proved to be a pretty seamless analogy to shopping for personal loan investments. In the future, I think we could test even more shopping patterns - like showing investment trends or comparing options.

WHAT I WOULD DO NEXT

There are a number of tweaks I'd like to make to this feature now that it's released. Here are a few:

  • Approachability And Learning. The experience right now is catered mainly to intermediate and experienced investors. With a few tweaks, we can simplify complex copy and provide guidance to get novice users started.
  • Personalization And Empathy. I explored a few different ways to make finance feel more human. With more fine-tuning, I think we can improve the personalization many of our investors appreciate.
  • Save For Later. I discovered that some investors prefer to shop, tentatively add investments to their cart, and come back later to purchase. Unfortunately, the app doesn't support this functionality today.
