Process: Content Planning

OVERVIEW

On a recent homepage overhaul project, I spent a good deal of time thinking about what kind of saved content should actually make the homepage itself. This small piece of process demonstrates how I used user behavior to prioritize content in a way that could be applicable to any homepage experience.

IN A USER'S SHOES

I created a rough first pass at what saved content should be immediately accessible (assuming there’s a great quantity). In a nutshell, I determined things that are iterated on or referenced frequently are the most crucial.

TIME TO SOCIALIZE

Once I’d gotten the essential structure of the decision tree down and discussed with various project stakeholders, it was time to socialize! I knew this process would be referenced in the future as new types of saved things were added, and I wanted others to be aware of my thought process. In a way, the tree could provide some self-governance. I planned to create a shareable print poster.

BRINGING IN BRAND

I decided to rebrand in the MasterCard style. I used MasterCard brand colors - white for questions and orange for decision points. I also added a background color to further distinguish between decision points. I went through several iterations before landing on one I liked.

RESULT

A solid foundation for deciding what should appear on the homepage in terms of saved work, and a visual method for communicating these decisions. With time, I’d like to play with a light theme and could probably increase stroke size.
