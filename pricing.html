Pricing Concepts

OVERVIEW

Client. Waffleonia, a large food chain.*

Task. Build an app that recommends price changes based on sales history in specific geographies.

Constraints. The app needed to integrate with an existing data suite.

My role. I led design from the ground up on a team that consisted of a project manager, QA, and five developers.

Result. The MVP was piloted and eventually rolled out to our client, Waffleonia.

*For privacy purposes, client name and images have been conceptualized.

Process Overview

Over the course of five months, the development team and me partnered closely with our client to define requirements, observe their activities, and obtain feedback.

MANAGING STAKEHOLDERS & EXPECTATIONS

The price setting process primarily involves Waffleonia executives, franchise owners, and, to a lesser extent, store managers. Most importantly, executives cannot actually set prices; the price setting power lies with franchise owners. This meant our app, used by executives, would have to persuasively appeal to franchise owners.

EARLY CONCEPTS

We produced early concept mockups to share with Waffleonia as part of our contract. Supported only by research and limited user testing, we produced screens representing two aspects of the app we knew would be essential: Settings and Results.

REFINING REQUIREMENTS

We continued to refine functional requirements before diving into UI or visual design tweaks. We conducted interviews with Waffleonia employees to inform our decisions and validated our choices with bi-weekly mockup walkthroughs.

First, we observed the complexity of the Waffleonia menu. Total menu size was manageable, ranging from 40-50 items, but many prices were dependent on one another. For instance, a small hot chocolate couldn’t cost more than a medium hot chocolate. We wanted our app to observe these rules but also provide flexibility.

Additionally, we observed Waffleonia employees maneuvering through sets of iterations on price recommendations before meeting with franchise owners. Because of business rules and constraints, our app had to provide room for modification as well.

USER TESTING

During testing of a design prototype onsite with Waffleonia, we made two discoveries:

  • The price recommendation process is collaborative and
  • Refining recommendations is iterative.

We pivoted slightly, loosening the linear workflow in favor of practical versioning and dynamic constraint editing. We moved the ability to save modifications as versions to the top level, given its importance in identifying and moving between versions. Now, recommendation sets could be identified by region and pricing strategy.

A HINT OF VISUAL THINKING

To support clean and meaningful data, we used color intentionally and sparingly. Color was used to indicate points of interest, as well as to signify deeper analytics. We experimented with a basic color palette and color placement.

RESULT

Our MVP was rolled out to Waffleonia and they loved it! Since beginning their pilot, five employees at Waffleonia have been onboarded, generating 100+ iterations for various regions and franchisees.

While the project did not receive continued development support, we’ve been able to pull patterns from the app, including versioning, to be used in other projects.
