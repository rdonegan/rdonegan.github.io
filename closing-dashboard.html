Order Summary

OVERVIEW

Context. Title agents use Qualia to manage orders across their business. The order hub provides a summary of an order’s progress.

Problem. Order processors have trouble assessing the status of an order at a glance.

Constraints. The most important aspect of the page - tracking milestones - was well established and could not be significantly altered.

My Role. I worked with our product team to identify customer challenges, work through iterations, and develop a final solution.

Result. We launched a revamped order hubpage for all clients and all order types.

Solution

Our overhaul of the order hub addressed our customers’ most pervasive concerns and was built to be customizable.

  1. The page is restructured to clearly communicate order status and progress
  2. Users can quickly adjust the most important orders stats - now positioned clearly below the progress indicator - by clicking and editing.
  3. Dashboard metrics can be customized by order type to accommodate differences in refinance, purchase, cash orders etc.
  4. Users can open a message between themselves and another Qualia user or another order contact by clicking on their name

Tracking Progress

Users can easily update the progress of an order by clicking on the progress tracker located at the top of the order hub.

Instant Messaging

Order processors can communicate efficiently with external contacts and colleagues via the platform's instant messaging feature, which allows for easy email kickoffs and in-app chat.

Order Customization

Users can prioritize the display of critical information that is most relevant to their specific order types. This feature is especially valuable for customers who process a large volume of order types.

THE PROBLEM

Our team noticed a few common problems on the hub page:

  1. Users had difficulty finding the right information quickly, resulting in excessive clicking and navigation.
  2. Order processors lacked clear methods for tracking and updating order progress.
  3. Customers were frustrated that the page was too tailored to purchases, neglecting other file types.

OUR PROCESS

Ultimately, the continuous feedback we received guided our work in a different, more customizable direction.

PRIORITIZING PROGRESS

Customers repeatedly emphasized the importance of tracking order progress. To address this, I had several goals:

  1. Streamline the page and use visual cues to make it easier to check order status quickly
  2. Simplify the progress bar controls to make it easier to adjust the status
  3. Allow users to update order status by clicking on the progress bar as they expected

One simple step to make the page instantly more intuitive? Make the existing progress bar interactive.

INFORMATION HIERARCHY

I assumed that most of our users were task-oriented, and I explored introducing new types of information on the page to this effect.

The feedback I received was stark:

  1. Users found the introduction of tasks redundant.
  2. Clients universally felt the multi-column layouts I shared were cluttered.

I responded to this feedback by reorienting the page in terms of what customers expected.

MESSAGING

After checking file progress, we found users often wanted to contact a teammate to followup. To make it easier to open a thread, the new layout allows users to click on a teammate or order contact to instantly open a chat or email thread.

BETA AND LAUNCH

After launching a small beta to a select group of small to medium-sized clients, we received early feedback that users desired more control over their summary page layout. Specifically, they wanted to be able to customize which statistics were displayed based on the type of file they were working on.

In response, we added a variety of customization options to our wide release, including the ability to rearrange the order of statistics and add or remove specific stats. We also updated error messaging and introducing support for subordinate orders. By working closely with users and taking an iterative approach to development, we successfully "refreshed" the hub experience without sacrificing critical functionality.
