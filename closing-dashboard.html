Order Summary

OVERVIEW

Context. Title agents use Qualia to track and manage orders across their business. The order hub provides a summary of an order’s progress and info.

Problem. Order processors had trouble assessing the status of an order at a glance, and had to search to find critical info like contact information and loan details.

Constraints. The most important aspect of the page - tracking milestones - was already well established and could not be altered in our page update.

My Role. I worked with our product team to identify customer challenges, work through iterations, and develop a final solution.

Result. We launched a revamped order hubpage for all clients and all order types.

Solution

Our overhaul of the order hub addressed our customers’ most pervasive concerns and was built to be customizable: .

  1. The page is restructured to clearly communicate order status and progress
  2. Users can quickly adjust the most important orders stats - now positioned clearly below the progress indicator - by clicking and editing.
  3. Dashboard metrics can be customized by order type to accommodate differences in refinance, purchase, cash orders etc.
  4. Users can open a message between themselves and another Qualia user or another order contact by clicking on their name

Tracking Progress

Users can easily update the progress of an order by clicking on the progress tracker located at the top of the order hub.

Instant Messaging

Order processors can communicate efficiently with external contacts and colleagues via the platform's instant messaging feature, which allows for easy email kickoffs and in-app chat.

Order Customization

Users can prioritize the display of critical information that is most relevant to their specific order types. This feature is especially valuable for customers who process a large volume of order types.

THE PROBLEM

Our team noticed that many customers were not fully utilizing the order hub page and often had to navigate back and forth to find what they needed. To address these issues, we set out to tackle several specific problems:

  1. Users had difficulty finding the right information quickly, resulting in excessive clicking and navigation.
  2. Order processors lacked clear methods for tracking and updating order progress.
  3. Customers expressed frustration that the page was too tailored to purchases, neglecting important information for other order types such as cash orders.

OUR PROCESS

For a high visibility project like this, it was important that we took advantage of direct client feedback at every step. Ultimately, the feedback we received took our work in a different, more customizable direction.

PRIORITIZING PROGRESS

Customers repeatedly emphasized the importance of tracking order progress. Through conversations with users, I learned that many relied on the order hub page to review and update each order's status. To address this, I had several goals:

  1. Streamline the page and use visual cues to make it easier to check order status quickly
  2. Simplify the progress bar controls to make it easier to adjust the status
  3. Allow users to update order status by clicking on the progress bar as they expected

During user testing, I observed that everyone tried to update the order status by clicking on the progress bar first. By making the progress bar interactive, I was able to match user expectations and simplify the controls on the page.

INFORMATION HIERARCHY

My early explorations involved a lot of play with information hierarchy on the page. I assumed that most of our users were task-oriented, and I explored introducing new types of information on the page to this effect.

The feedback I received was stark:

  1. Users did not appreciate the introduction of tasks on the hub page. Instead, they found it redundant and out-of-place
  2. The multi-column layouts I shared did not help to simplify the page. Some customers observed that the page felt more cluttered than before, but now some of the information was less relevant.

I responded to this feedback by reorienting the page in terms of what customers expected, and prioritized that hierarchically.

MESSAGING

After reviewing the status of an order via the dashboard, users often want to reach out to a contact for more details or followup. To make it easier to open a thread, our new layout allows users to click on a teammate or order contact to instantly open an internal chat or email thread. Crucially, the UI supports multiple team chats open at a time

BETA AND LAUNCH

After launching a small beta to a select group of small to medium-sized clients, we received early feedback that users desired more control over their summary page layout. Specifically, they wanted to be able to customize which statistics were displayed based on the type of order they were working on (e.g. purchases, refinances, HELOCs, etc.).

In response, we added a variety of customization options to our wide release, including the ability to rearrange the order of statistics, add or remove specific stats, and more. We also made additional improvements based on user feedback, such as updating error messaging and introducing support for subordinate orders. By working closely with users and taking an iterative approach to design, we were able to create a successful product that was well-received by clients supporting a variety of closing options.
